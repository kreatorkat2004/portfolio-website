  • Uncovering Biases in California Mortgage Data (2023-2024)

    Investigated potential biases in California mortgage loan data from 2016, focusing on race, income level, and sex.

    Explored disparities in mortgage lending practices using data from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and county-level Gini Index measures.

    Analyzed over 1 million observations to identify correlations between loan amounts and applicant demographics.

    Found a positive correlation between applicant income and loan amount, highlighting income disparities among different racial groups.

    Discovered that Asian and White applicants received higher loan amounts, correlated with higher income levels.

    Observed a positive correlation between county Gini Index and mean loan amount, with higher minority percentages correlating with higher loan amounts in more unequal counties.

    Concluded that differences in loan amounts were primarily income-related, suggesting the need for further research with more representative data.
